# Platform: Blog

Long-form writing for Aswin's personal blog/website. This is where ideas get full room to breathe — narrative arc, nuance, and exploration are welcome here.

## Characteristics

- **Length:** 800-2,500 words (sweet spot: 1,200-1,800)
- **Tone:** Most reflective and nuanced of all platforms
- **Pipeline:** Full path with all 5 editorial passes + critic loop
- **Audience:** People who chose to click through — they are already interested. Reward that interest.

## Structure Options

### The Essay (Most Common)
Strong opening (tension, question, or observation) → development through experience and evidence → the turn (the insight) → the close (something worth sitting with).

Works for: reflections on leadership, career lessons, industry observations.

### The Argument
Thesis stated clearly → supporting points with evidence → anticipated counterarguments addressed → conclusion that reframes the thesis with earned authority.

Works for: contrarian takes, "here's what everyone gets wrong about X."

### The Narrative
Scene-setting → rising tension → the moment of realization → what changed → the takeaway.

Works for: failure stories, career pivots, lessons from specific projects.

### The Technical Deep-Dive
Problem statement → approach taken → the nuance most miss → the takeaway for practitioners.

Works for: technical topics made accessible, lessons from building systems.

## Formatting

- Headers and subheaders allowed (unlike social posts)
- Code blocks when illustrating technical points
- Bold sparingly for key phrases
- Pull quotes for the most important single sentence
- No more than 3-4 headers in a typical post — more than that and the piece is a listicle in disguise

## Opening Rules

Blog openings can be slower than social — scene-setting is acceptable. But the first paragraph must still create a reason to keep reading. Not a summary. Not "In this post I will discuss." A moment of tension, a surprising observation, or a question the reader now wants answered.

### Openings That Work
- A specific moment: "Last Tuesday, our most important dashboard went down. Nobody noticed for three days."
- A counterintuitive claim: "The best data team I ever built had zero data scientists."
- A question: "What happens when the model is right but the decision is wrong?"

### Openings to Avoid
- "I've been thinking a lot about..." (filler)
- "In today's world of..." (generic setup)
- Meta-commentary about the post itself

## Closing Rules

Blog closings should not summarize. The reader just read the piece — they do not need a recap. Instead:

- **Circle back** to the opening with new understanding
- **End on a specific image or moment** — concrete, not abstract
- **Leave a question** that the reader takes with them
- **One sentence that lands** — quotable, memorable, earned

## SEO Considerations

- Title: clear, specific, under 60 characters. The title should work as a promise.
- First paragraph should contain the core topic naturally (not keyword-stuffed)
- Headers should be scannable — a reader skimming headers should get the argument's shape
- Meta description: 1-2 sentences that make someone click. Not a summary — a hook.

## Voice Calibration for Blog

Blog Aswin is the most exploratory version. He can:
- Work through an idea in real time, showing the thinking process
- Be more vulnerable and reflective than on social
- Use longer sentences when they earn their length
- Include uncertainty — "I am not sure about this, but..."
- Go deeper into technical concepts with full context

He should NOT:
- Lecture or preach
- Write documentation disguised as a blog post
- Use the blog as a LinkedIn post with more paragraphs
- Pad for word count — if the idea is 800 words, do not stretch to 1,500

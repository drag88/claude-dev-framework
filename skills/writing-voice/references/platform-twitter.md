# Platform: Twitter/X

Twitter rewards density, wit, and punchy insight. Every word must earn its place. The constraint of brevity forces clarity.

## Characteristics

- **Length:** Single tweet 180-280 chars, threads 5-8 tweets
- **Tone:** Sharpest of all platforms. More opinionated. Conversational. Intellectually playful.
- **Pipeline:** Fast path (single tweets), Full path (threads only)
- **Audience:** Fast-scrolling. You get one shot. Make it count.

## Pipeline for Twitter

- **Single tweets:** Use Compression method from generative-methods.md. Self-review only. No editorial pipeline.
- **Threads:** Full path with Minto (thread-level argument) + McPhee (thread arc) + Zinsser (per-tweet density) + Forsyth (thread closer). Critic loop optional.

## Voice Guides for Twitter

### Naval's Style: Philosophical Density

**Definition:** "X is Y" — clean, declarative, reframeable.
- "Wealth is assets that earn while you sleep."
- "Specific knowledge is knowledge you cannot be trained for."

**Contrast:** "Not X, but Y" — challenges assumptions.
- "Seek wealth, not money or status."
- "Code and media are permissionless leverage. Capital and labor are permissioned."

**Chain:** Linking concepts that build.
- "Learn to sell. Learn to build. If you can do both, you will be unstoppable."

**Reframe:** Common belief twisted.
- "Building specific knowledge will feel like play to you but will look like work to others."

**First principles:** Stripping to fundamentals.
- "All the returns in life come from compound interest."

**Use when:** Philosophical or principle-based insight. Defining concepts. Tweetstorms.

### Karpathy's Style: Technical Wit

**Self-deprecating observation:** Finding absurdity in technical work.
- Humor about shared practitioner experiences.

**Casual profundity:** Deep insight delivered conversationally.

**Shared experience:** "Everyone who does X knows this feeling."

**Surprising analogy:** Unexpected parallel that illuminates.

**Build-in-public moment:** Sharing work as it happens, rough edges included.

**Use when:** Technical topics. Self-awareness. Sharing work or learnings. Making complexity accessible.

### The Blend

| Naval Brings | Karpathy Brings | The Blend |
|---|---|---|
| Philosophical depth | Technical credibility | Insights grounded in real work |
| Definitional clarity | Playful self-awareness | Accessible wisdom |
| First principles | Practical experience | Theory meeting reality |
| Timeless aphorisms | Timely observations | Relevant but lasting |

### Technical-Philosophical Synthesis

Technical concepts that reveal deeper truths. The technical foundation must be accurate — the philosophy emerges from it, not imposed on it.

**Structure:**
```
[Technical concept stated accurately]
[The deeper implication beyond the technical domain]
[Optional: "In X. And in Y."]
```

**Examples:**
- "Gradient descent finds the local minimum, not the global one. To escape, you need noise. Optimization without disruption leads to mediocre convergence. In ML. And in life."
- "In distributed systems, the hardest problems aren't the nodes — they're the connections between them. Same with teams."
- "Technical debt isn't about code. It's deferred honesty."

**Domains to mine:** Optimization, systems design, information theory, database concepts, software patterns, ML fundamentals.

**Pitfalls:** Do not force philosophy onto technical concepts that do not support it. Do not get the technical part wrong. Do not over-explain the metaphor. Trust the reader.

## Single Tweet Structures

- **Observation:** Specific insight stated cleanly. No preamble.
- **Reframe:** Common belief stated, then twisted.
- **Confession:** Vulnerability compressed. No self-pity.
- **Pattern:** Something you have noticed, stated as pattern.
- **Question:** Genuine curiosity, not engagement bait.
- **Counter-consensus:** Disagree with something widely accepted. Back it up.

## Thread Structure

### Thread Opening (Tweet 1)
Hook strong enough to make people want the rest. Either start with the punchline or create irresistible curiosity.

### Thread Body (Tweets 2-7)
Each tweet: stands alone as a complete thought, builds on previous, adds genuine value (no filler tweets). Keep threads tight: 5-8 tweets ideal. Beyond 10, quality drops.

### Thread Close
Land it: summarize core insight, end memorably. No call to action — let the content speak.

## Formatting

- Line breaks to separate distinct thoughts within a tweet
- Bold or caps sparingly for ONE key word
- No more than 1-2 emojis
- No excessive punctuation
- No hashtags in body (one at end if necessary)
- Do not start threads with "Thread:" or "1/" — just start
- Quote-tweet with added perspective, not just "This."

## Opening Lines

**Works:**
- Observation (insight-first): "Every time I see [X], I notice [Y]..."
- Direct insight: No preamble, just the idea.
- Counter: "[Common belief] is incomplete."
- Pattern: "Same pattern keeps emerging:"
- Genuine curiosity: "Why do we [common behavior] when [it doesn't work]?"

**Avoid:**
- "Let me tell you why..."
- "Thread:" or thread emoji
- "Unpopular opinion:"
- "Friendly reminder that..."
- "Can we talk about..."
- Any opener about YOU rather than the IDEA

## Before Publishing

1. Can this be said in fewer words? (If yes, cut)
2. Genuine insight or just noise?
3. Would I engage with this if someone else posted it?
4. Sounds like a human thinking out loud?
5. Zero cringe-worthy phrases?
6. If thread, does each tweet earn its place?
